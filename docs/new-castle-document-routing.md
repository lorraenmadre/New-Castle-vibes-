# New Castle Document Routing

New Castle is the legal and document shell for the Lorraen Madre UFO system.

Its job is to receive documents, identify what they are, extract useful information, and route them into the correct WISH WELL house, folder, task board, or output path.

## Purpose

New Castle exists to make documents usable.

It should help turn scattered files into:

- Summaries
- Timelines
- Tasks
- Drafts
- House assignments
- Monday.com items
- Google Drive outputs
- Human review packets

## Core Routing Questions

Every document should answer:

1. What type of document is this?
2. Who or what does it involve?
3. What dates matter?
4. What obligations or deadlines appear?
5. Which WISH WELL house does it belong to?
6. What task should be created?
7. Where should the document live?
8. Does it need human review?

## Intake Categories

Early routing categories:

- Court document
- Contract or agreement
- Trust or governance document
- Entity document
- Insurance document
- Financial record
- Health or benefits record
- Housing or property document
- Grant document
- Business plan
- Operating agreement
- Intake form

## Routing Output

Each routed document should produce:

- One summary
- One primary WISH WELL house assignment
- Optional secondary house assignment
- Key facts
- Key dates
- People or entities involved
- Missing information
- Recommended next action
- Storage destination
- Monday.com task recommendation, if needed

## Example

Document: insurance policy

Routing:

- Primary house: House 8
- Secondary house: House 2, if money or asset value is involved
- Output: policy summary, coverage notes, renewal date, risk checklist
- Monday.com task: Review policy before renewal

## Storage Rule

Documents may start in New Castle intake, but final outputs should also be routed to the matching WISH WELL house when useful.

New Castle is the intake and processing shell.

The WISH WELL vault is the long-term operating map.

## Outcome

New Castle should prevent documents from becoming a haunted drawer of PDFs.

Every file should have a name, a house, a summary, a task if needed, and a place to live.
